<p><p>The Anti-IgG antibody market comprises two main types: monoclonal and polyclonal antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are uniform, produced from a single clone of B cells, offering specificity and consistency for research and clinical applications. In contrast, polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple B cell clones, resulting in a heterogeneous mix that can recognize various epitopes on the IgG target. Both types play critical roles in diagnostics, therapeutics, and research, catering to different needs in scientific and medical fields.</p></p>
