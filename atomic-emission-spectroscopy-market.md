<p><p>The Atomic Emission Spectroscopy market includes several types based on the excitation source used. Flame Atomic Emission Spectroscopy utilizes flame to vaporize samples and excite atoms. Spark Atomic uses electrical sparks to induce emission, while Arc Atomic employs an electric arc for excitation. Inductively Coupled Plasma Atomic Spectroscopy involves plasma generated by electromagnetic fields to ionize samples, providing high sensitivity. Additionally, the "Others" segment encompasses various emerging and specialized techniques that cater to specific applications or sample types in analytical chemistry.</p></p>
