<p><p>The polio vaccine market comprises two primary types: Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV) and Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV). IPV is administered via injection and contains inactivated virus, offering strong individual immunity without the risk of causing disease. In contrast, OPV is given orally and contains live attenuated virus, promoting community immunity through viral shedding but posing a rare risk of vaccine-derived poliovirus. Both vaccines play crucial roles in global eradication efforts, addressing different immunization strategies and population needs.</p></p>
