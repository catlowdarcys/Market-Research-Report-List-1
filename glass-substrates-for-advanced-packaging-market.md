<p><p>Glass substrates for advanced packaging are categorized based on their Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE), which measures how much a material expands with temperature changes. Substrates with a CTE above 5 ppm/°C are typically used in environments with higher thermal stresses, providing flexibility in applications. Conversely, substrates with a CTE below 5 ppm/°C offer superior dimensional stability, making them ideal for high-precision applications where thermal cycling is a critical factor. Both types cater to different technological requirements in microelectronics and packaging.</p></p>
